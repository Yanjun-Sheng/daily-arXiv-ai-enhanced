### [1] [Collisional Dynamics of Stars and Dark Matter in Ultra-Faint Galaxies](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.06304)
*Raphaël Errani,Nicolas Esser,Jorge Peñarrubia,Matthew G. Walker*

Main category: astro-ph.GA

TL;DR: Stars exchange energy with dark matter via dynamical friction, transforming dark matter cusps into cores and creating dense, baryon-dominated stellar clusters in ultra-faint galaxies.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To understand how collisional energy exchange between stars and dark matter particles affects the structure and evolution of ultra-faint galaxies, particularly the transformation of dark matter profiles and formation of baryon-dominated systems.

Method: Controlled N-body simulations studying dynamical friction between stars and subsolar-mass dark matter particles in ultra-faint galaxies, focusing on tidally limited systems with low stellar velocity dispersion.

Result: Dynamical friction depletes dark matter from galaxy centers, transforming cusps into constant-density cores. High-mass stars sink toward halo centers, decreasing dynamical-to-stellar mass ratio within half-light radius, forming dense baryon-dominated clusters. Collisional cooling facilitates stellar binary formation, eventually slowing contraction due to reduced central dark matter densities and binary formation.

Conclusion: Energy exchange between stars and dark matter creates a rich phenomenology in ultra-faint galaxies, blurring the distinction between globular cluster and galaxy dynamics, potentially explaining dark matter-poor systems with ultra-faint galaxy chemical compositions.

Abstract: We use controlled N-body simulations to study the collisional exchange of energy between stars and dark matter in ultra-faint galaxies. We find that dynamical friction between stars and subsolar-mass dark matter particles results in the depletion of dark matter from the galaxies' centers, thereby transforming dark matter cusps into constant-density cores. The process is particularly effective in tidally limited galaxies with low stellar velocity dispersion. As high-mass stars sink toward the center of the dark matter halo, the dynamical-to-stellar mass ratio within the stellar half-light radius decreases monotonically. The stellar population of a dark matter-dominated galaxy is thereby compacted into a dense, baryon-dominated cluster, surrounded by a dark matter halo. Such a cluster would share the chemical composition of an ultra-faint galaxy, yet would be virtually dark matter-free within its half-light radius. We moreover find that the collisional cooling with dark matter particles provides an efficient pathway for the formation of stellar binaries in the contracting cluster. The contraction is eventually slowed down due to the decreasing central dark matter densities and the formation of stellar binaries. Our models highlight that the dynamical processes governing the faintest galaxies give rise to a rich phenomenology, blurring the line between the dynamics of globular clusters and galaxies.
